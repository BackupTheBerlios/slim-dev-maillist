Comment:
Well, SLiM doesn't really depend on ImageMagick. It's just shipped with a default configuration which uses ImageMagick. Personally I think this is a good thing because:
1) The default configuration should be "easy" and working out-of-the-box and ImageMagick is, whether we like it or not, a standard tool in many distroes.
2) Any distro is free to alter the configuration file they ship to fit their infrastructure and philosophy. So a minimalistic distro might choose your solution; but that is their choice, not ours.

That said, I think we could include your idea in the documentation. I'll discuss it with the other devs.
