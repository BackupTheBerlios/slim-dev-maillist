> I just wanted to know, how to perform a clean uninstall of SLiM under
> Linux, because I want to reinstall in a bit a different way.
Well, if it's not installed using a package manager (which I'd say is
the standard way on Linux) but from source, you can simply undo what the
'install' and 'install-theme'. There's no 'uninstall' target at this
point in time.

Basically, that's removing
$(DESTDIR)$(PREFIX)/bin/slim
$(DESTDIR)$(MANDIR)/man1/slim.1
$(DESTDIR)$(CFGDIR)/slim.conf    (you may want to keep that)
$(DESTDIR)$(PREFIX)/share/slim/themes/default/slim.theme
$(DESTDIR)$(PREFIX)/share/slim/themes/default/panel.png
$(DESTDIR)$(PREFIX)/share/slim/themes/default/background.jpg

If you installed it via a plain 'make install', $(DESTDIR) will be
empty, $(PREFIX) will be /usr, and CFGDIR /etc.

Let me know if you have further questions on that.
